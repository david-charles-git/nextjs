Development period

Instead of designing a boot only to find out it wasn’t possible to make it eco-friendly, we reverse engineered a boot and packed as many eco-friendly materials into the shoe as possible. Each material factored in Performance, Planet and Price to give us the optimal performance boot.

Tech specs

Weight: 214g (comparatively speaking, this is similar to a Nike Mercurial, the most recognisable lightweight speed boot on the market)
Soleplate: FG (Firm ground) - For hard, dry grass and artificial turf surfaces.
SG (Soft ground) - Made for the UK’s wintery months, when playing surfaces are more of a swamp than a pitch. (Soft, Muddy and wet grass surfaces).

Non-slip grip

Our eco-suede lining is 85% recycled polyester, sourced from discarded plastic bottles. The material has a suede type texture, for a super soft ‘slipper-like’ touch and a non-slip grippy texture, to lock the foot into place.

Castor bean soleplate

Today, castor oil helps us to create sustainably produced plastic. Traditionally, your tupperware would be made from fossil fuels (unrefined petrol), which creates a shedload of CO2. But castor oil creates a renewable carbon variant without the nasty side effects. Castor oil is a feedstock that takes less than one year to grow and does not compete with the human or animal food chain. It is inherently drought resistant, requiring little irrigation or fertilisation. The percentage of renewable carbon is 87 - 91%.

Soft ground studs

Sokito was born in the UK, so we understand the need for grip when the pitch turns to a swamp. Our soft ground studs are produced from 100% aluminium, that’s all.
Aluminium fact of the day… 75% of all of the aluminium ever produced is still in circulation. It can be melted down and re-used countless times, which makes it perfect for the studs in the Devista.

Eco-foam insole

Sokito’s insoles are produced using eco-foam. Super lightweight with exceptional rebound properties, they’ll have you feeling like you’re running on clouds. Eco-foam contains 20% recycled rubber.

Stitching thread

Talk about an unsung hero. Stitching thread is rarely noticed, but without it, football boots would be nothing more than a pile of material. We eco-analysed every component in the Devista and replaced the traditional polyester thread with an alternative made from 100% recycled plastic bottles.

Heel Counter

A heel counter is a vital piece in the football boot puzzle. It provides structure and protection to the heel area and can play a vital part in the overall comfort of the boot. Ours sits between the upper and the lining and is made with TPE (40% of that being recycled). We have limited traceability on this material at the moment and are working on ways to certify the origin of the raw material.

Eco-suede lining

Recycled polyester is sourced from recycled PET bottles, which are collected, cleaned and spun into new polyester fibre. The lining in each pair contains 1.08 recycled bottles.

Engineered lacing system

Off-centre lacing opens surface area on the medial side of the shoe (the strike zone), allowing for cleaner contact,higher levels of control and greater shot power.

Comfort toe padding

The ‘package’ (combination of materials) in the toe area has been specifically designed to optimise both comfort and performance, with a mix of soft upper materials and strategically placed padding and protection.

Crafted by hand

European production allows for tighter controls on quality and employee working conditions, whilst minimising supply chain emissions. Our handmade production methods allow us to maximise material optimisation, ensuring less waste materials.

Eco-lastic tongue strap

Okay, it’s a small detail. And yes, it’s only on one of our boots. But every little detail matters to us at Sokito.
The elastic that holds down your fold over tongue is made with 70% recycled plastic bottles and 30% spandex (to make it stretchy).
Each pair contains 0.37 recycled bottles.

Eco-laces

Every Sokito shoelace is produced using 100% recycled polyester, from discarded plastic bottles. Each pair contains 1.09 recycled bottles, with the lace tips made from plant based cellulose too.

Upper reinforcement

The upper reinforcement is made from cellulose, which consists of 100% waste paper fibres. Collected from sustainable wood sources, we use the material to reinforce our boots. Its strength prevents overstretching but stays soft to move and mould to your feet. You’re protected, even if you can’t see it.

Kangaroo leather vamp

Kangaroo leather is tough-as-nails, which is excellent for durability. It’s also incredibly soft, which ensures a comfortable and flexible fit. Our kangaroo leather comes from only wild kangaroos down under and is a bi-product of governmental population control which is enforced to protect local agriculture. Most brands hide behind the ‘K-leather’ term, not us. All tannage and material dyes are REACH compliant and comply with ecological and toxicological criteria for the EU ecolabel award. All scrap leather, from both supplier and Sokito production is recycled into fertiliser.

Cotton vamp reinforcement

Unless you’re like us and spend your days taking apart football boots, you likely won’t know that this component of a boot exists.
Football boots often require reinforcement in the vamp (toe area) to prevent overstretching. In leather boots, this is usually virgin nylon (not very good for mother nature). Sokito switched the plastic for a natural cotton material (better for mother nature). Right now we have limited traceability on the raw material but we’re working on ways to guarantee that all of our cotton is organic (best for mother nature).

Eco-suede insole top cloth

Our insoles are topped with a non-slip eco-suede, which is made up of 51% recycled polyester, produced from discarded plastic bottles.
The material locks the foot into place to prevent slipping and improve grip to help with push-off speed
Each pair contains 0.41 recycled bottles (size 9)

Innovation

As a planet positive brand, we’re committed to transparency. Here are the materials that we currently have to use but are working hard to find alternatives for:Stud tips – Virgin TPU – To guarantee abrasion resistance.Lasting insole – Polypropylene – To avoid stud pressure.Eyestay reinforcement - Fibreglass – To make sure lace holes don’t tear.Foam padding – Virgin materials – For consistent softness and rebound.Adhesive – Solvent based – To keep the boot in one piece.
